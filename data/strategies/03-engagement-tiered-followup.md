# Strategy: Engagement-Tiered Follow-up (+7 days)

**When to use:** touch #2 ONLY, at +7 days, for any prospect not flagged replied. This
is the follow-up strategy whose effectiveness the experiment is explicitly measuring
(spec 004, FR-016b). The engagement tier from touch #1 *shapes* the angle; it never
*gates* eligibility (open tracking is noisy — Apple MPP / Gmail proxy phantom opens,
images-off false non-opens).

**Tier → angle:**
- `clicked` → strongest signal. Be more direct: reference that they looked, make a
  concrete next-step ask (a specific time, a sample, a spec sheet).
- `opened` → light nudge. One new proof point or a sharper one-line value frame; soft CTA.
- `silent` → different angle entirely. Switch strategy (e.g. supply-div → TCO), new
  subject line, do not repeat touch #1's framing.

**Grounding:** inherit the touch-#1 KB anchors; add at most one new anchored claim.

**Structure:**
- Subject: NEW (never "Re:" a fabricated thread unless genuinely threaded to the original send).
- Body: 2–4 lines max — shorter than touch #1. Acknowledge it's a follow-up without apologizing.
- Close: one ask, lower friction than touch #1.

**Norms:** ≤ 60 words. Threads to the original send. Capped — there is no touch #3 in
the pilot. Every follow-up still passes the morning approval gate.

**Why it works (research):** most replies to cold sequences come on the follow-up;
tiering the angle to the strongest observed signal beats a blind "just bumping this".
