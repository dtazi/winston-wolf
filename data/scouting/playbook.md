# Scouting playbook — the question template

**Status: DRAFT** (pending operator sign-off on the questions). This is the
reasoning method the AI works through to scout, co-pilot now and the blueprint
for an automated Scout tool later. The point of the playbook is that the
*questions* are explicit and improvable — we refine them as we learn.

## Step 1 — Demand mapping (which industries to pursue)
1. What end-markets buy institutional-grade mattresses/bedding at volume?
   (healthcare, hospitality, education, government/corrections, military, …)
2. For each: US market size / annual unit volume + replacement cycle (re-buy
   frequency). **Source every number.**
3. Procurement model: internal procurement team vs outsourced. Where outsourced
   (GPOs, FF&E procurement firms, dealers/distributors, contract furnishers),
   add the outsourcer as its own targetable segment and rate it.
4. Buying triggers per segment (new build, renovation/refresh cycle, supply-chain
   disruption, regulation/standards).
5. Richbond fit: where is Richbond *most credible* (China-alternative, Morocco-US
   FTA / TAA-compliant, 60-yr institutional manufacturer, owns Simmons/Beautyrest
   ops, Silentnight in Kenya)?
6. Reachability: are the decision-makers cold-emailable (clear titles, findable
   addresses)?
→ **Output:** a ranked shortlist of 2–3 segments to pursue first, with rationale.

## Step 2 — Acquisition strategy (how to find the companies, per chosen segment)
7. Best channels to enumerate companies in this segment? (import/customs records,
   trade-show exhibitor lists, LinkedIn, public directories, RFP/bid databases,
   GPO membership, "what they buy/sell" we can track, media.)
8. Rank channels by (a) company yield and (b) email-difficulty from that point.
9. Pick the lead channel(s); enumerate companies; identify the right role/title.
→ **Output:** a target company list + the contact-finding plan per company.

## Discipline (every run)
- State sources for factual claims; flag the unsourced (mirrors Art 17 for the KB).
- End each conclusion with what would change it / where it might be wrong.
- After each run, update `methods.md` (what worked) and `lessons.md` (why).
