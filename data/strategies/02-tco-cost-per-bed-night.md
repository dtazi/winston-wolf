# Strategy: Total Cost of Ownership / Cost-per-Bed-Night

**When to use:** decision-maker is procurement-led (Procurement Manager, Supply-Chain,
Category Manager, GPO contact) — someone who is measured on lifetime cost, not unit
price. Pairs well as the touch-#2 angle after a durability-led touch #1.

**Core angle:** commodity imports look cheaper per unit and cost more per bed-night
over their life because institutions cycle bedding 3–5× faster than residential. Richbond
is built for that load, so the lifetime math favors it.

**Grounding (KB anchors required):** `tco`, `durability`, `vertical-integration`.
Do NOT cite a specific cost figure or lifespan unless it exists in the KB — frame the
logic, not invented numbers.

**Structure:**
- Subject: the metric, e.g. "Cost-per-bed-night, not sticker price"
- Opener: name the procurement reality (replacement frequency under institutional load).
- Body: the TCO logic in one or two lines; one durability proof from the KB.
- Close: offer the comparison, e.g. "Worth a 15-min look at the lifetime math?"

**Norms:** ≤ 90 words. No price quotes (TCO framing only — KB hard guard). One CTA.

**Why it works (research):** procurement intermediaries and institutional buyers
optimize lifetime cost; the replacement-cycle pain (hotels ~5–8 yr, PIP-driven refresh)
is the lever this strategy pulls.
