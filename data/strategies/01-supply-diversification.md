# Strategy: Supply Diversification (China + 1)

**When to use:** prospect's segment or recent signals show China-tariff exposure,
lead-time pain, or an active second-source mandate. Strongest default angle for
Richbond — it leads with the one thing that is structurally true and hard to copy
(Morocco origin), not with a product claim.

**Core angle:** Richbond is the Morocco answer to "we need a non-China bedding source
that won't get hit by tariffs or 8-week ocean variance." Geography is the hook, the
product is the follow-through.

**Grounding (KB anchors required):** `china-plus-one`, `vertical-integration`, and —
only for federal/VA/military prospects — `taa-designated` (never `taa-conditional` as
a promise; state eligibility as conditional, not automatic).

**Structure:**
- Subject: concrete and specific, no hype. e.g. "Morocco-made bedding, no Section 301 exposure"
- Opener: one line naming the prospect's likely exposure (the research's `signals`), not about us.
- Body: one proof line (origin + vertical integration), one credibility line.
- Close: a single low-friction CTA — a question or a 15-min call, never "buy".

**Norms:** ≤ 90 words. One ask. No price. No more than one claim that needs the KB.
Plain text, no images except the tracking pixel. Reference accounts only if KB-confirmed.

**Why it works (research):** US buyers are actively seeking China alternatives; Morocco's
FTA + TAA status is a genuine, verifiable differentiator most competitors can't match.
